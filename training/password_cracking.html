Password Cracking

Real incidents, failures, and defensive lessons

What Is Password Cracking?

Password cracking is the process of recovering user passwords by guessing, deriving, or reusing credentials obtained from breaches.

  • Primary cause of account takeover
  • Scales rapidly with weak passwords
  • Exploited across email, cloud, banking, and admin systems

How Hackers Take Over Accounts

  • Steal password databases or hashes
  • Crack hashes offline using GPUs
  • Reuse leaked credentials across platforms
  • Escalate access to sensitive systems

Timeline of Major Password-Related Breaches

2009 – RockYou: 32M plaintext passwords leaked
2013 – Adobe: 153M weakly protected passwords exposed
2014 – Sony Pictures: Password reuse enabled lateral movement
2016 – LinkedIn: Unsalted SHA-1 hashes cracked
2018 – Marriott International: Long-term credential abuse
