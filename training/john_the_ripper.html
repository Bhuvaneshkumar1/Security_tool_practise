John the Ripper

Offline hash cracking — theory & safe simulation

What Is John the Ripper?

John the Ripper is an offline password cracking tool that attempts to recover plaintext passwords from password hashes after a breach.

Common Hash Types John Cracks

MD5

  • Size: 128-bit
  • Speed: Extremely fast
  • Crack time: Seconds–minutes

SHA-1

  • Size: 160-bit
  • Speed: Very fast
  • Crack time: Minutes–hours

SHA-256 / SHA-512

  • Speed: Fast
  • Crack time: Hours–days

NTLM

  • Used by: Windows
  • Speed: Extremely fast
  • Crack time: Seconds

bcrypt

  • Speed: Very slow
  • Crack time: Months–years

Real-World Breach Timeline (Offline Cracking Impact)

2009 – RockYou

Passwords stored in plaintext. Massive dataset later used with John to crack millions of reused passwords across other services.

2012 – LinkedIn

Unsalted SHA-1 hashes. Offline cracking recovered over 100 million passwords within days.

2013 – Adobe

Weak encryption and password hints allowed rule-based cracking using John-style attacks.

2013–2014 – Yahoo

MD5 hashing enabled long-term offline cracking and account takeovers.

2015 – Ashley Madison

bcrypt used, but weak passwords and low cost factors still allowed partial cracking.
